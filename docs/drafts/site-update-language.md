# Site update language: *Where This Is*

**Status:** SKETCH
**Triggers when:** The Trust's Articles of Incorporation are filed with WA Secretary of State.
**Replaces:** The second-to-last paragraph of the *Where This Is* section on dtlh.org.

## Draft language

> *Downtown Loyal Heights Trust, Inc.* is a Washington nonprofit corporation, formed [DATE], EIN [XX-XXXXXXX]. Its sole current activity is to plan, in conversation with neighbors, anchors, and counsel, whether and how a community-owned redevelopment of the corner of NW 80th and 24th Ave NW could be financed and built. No securities have been offered. No money is being accepted from any prospective investor. Tax-deductible donations to support predevelopment costs (legal, design, feasibility) can be made to [Fiscal Sponsor] earmarked for the DTLH project; we will be our own fiscal home once the IRS recognizes us under 501(c)(3).

## Notes

- Keep the existing ALL-CAPS disclaimer at the bottom of the page intact. This language adds entity status without weakening the "this is a concept, not an offering" posture.
- The fiscal sponsor name will be filled in once we've engaged one (Sustainable Seattle, SEED Seattle, or 501 Commons are likely candidates).
- The EIN should be live by the time this language is published — applying for EIN takes ~10 minutes online once Articles are filed.
- "Sole current activity is to plan" is deliberate phrasing. It signals to readers (and any regulator who might glance at the page) that the Trust is not yet operating any business, holding any investor capital, or making any legally binding commitments.

## Open questions

- Should we add a sentence linking to [`/docs/`](https://github.com/jedsundwall/dtlh/tree/main/docs) for transparency? Probably yes, but as a footer link or a separate paragraph rather than embedded here.
- Should we mention the LLC at all? Probably not until it's actually formed. Mentioning a planned-but-not-yet-formed entity invites misreading.
