# East Bay Permanent Real Estate Cooperative (EB PREC)

**Where:** Oakland, CA
**Founded:** 2017
**Legal form:** California cooperative corporation; incubated by the Sustainable Economies Law Center (SELC) and the People of Color Sustainable Housing Network
**Why we care:** Their SEC Form 1-A filing is the most complete public US filing for a community real estate cooperative — effectively a free template for offering circular disclosures, share-class definitions, and lock-up provisions.

## Brief

EB PREC officially incorporated in 2017 as a California cooperative corporation with three classes of owner-members: **Resident Owners**, **Community Owners**, and **Investor Owners**. The first capital raise was a California-only intrastate offering that brought in **$160,000 from 160 investors** — no SEC filing needed because the offering was restricted to California residents.

EB PREC subsequently filed a Reg A+ Direct Public Offering with the SEC and became the first PREC to qualify nationally. The Reg A+ filing authorized up to **$72M** (72,000 shares at $1,000 each); by 2023 they had raised about $5M via the DPO.

The structure leans heavily on California's purpose-built securities exemption for cooperatives — **AB 816 (2015)**, which SELC drafted and lobbied through the legislature specifically to enable community real estate cooperatives. Membership shares can be sold up to $1,000 each under California Corporations Code § 25100(o)/(r) without securities registration.

Investor share lock-ups are a minimum of five years; shares can only be sold back to the cooperative, not to third parties. Target dividend: 1.5%.

## What we're borrowing

- **The SEC Form 1-A filing as a drafting template.** This is the single most useful public artifact for any community real estate cooperative or fund. The offering circular covers share-class definitions, lock-up provisions, governance, and disclosure language at a level of detail that would cost tens of thousands to draft from scratch. Securities counsel should start from the EB PREC filing.
- **The two-stage securities path.** Stage 1: state-only intrastate offering (no SEC), small dollar amount, proves the model. Stage 2: Reg A+ for national reach. This is the right ladder for any community real estate vehicle that wants to scale beyond a single state. We can adapt: WA intrastate first for the small voting tranche, then Reg CF or Reg A+ for the larger community equity.
- **Three owner-member classes.** Resident, Community, and Investor — different share rights, different eligibility, different roles. Our Class V / Class N / Class A maps onto their architecture though with different naming and different specific rights.
- **Lock-up + buyback-only.** Shares can only be sold back to the cooperative, with a five-year minimum hold. Limits speculation, reinforces long-term commitment, simplifies cap table management. Worth replicating in our LLC operating agreement for Class N.

## What's different in our case

- **No California AB 816 equivalent in Washington.** This is the central reason we're forming a nonprofit + LLC rather than a cooperative. Washington's cooperative securities exemption ([RCW 21.20.320(15)](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=21.20.310) / WAC 460-44A-200) is narrower than California's, and there's no purpose-built community real estate provision. The nonprofit + LLC pattern works around this.
- **Operational model.** EB PREC's primary use case is acquiring and stabilizing existing residential and small commercial properties. We're contemplating a ground-up redevelopment of a specific corner. The asset-acquisition tempo is different.
- **Scale.** EB PREC's Reg A+ ceiling is $72M but they had raised $5M by 2023. Our Phase 2 may need to absorb a larger community equity tranche faster. Reg A+ Tier 2 (up to $75M) is in scope but the disclosure burden is heavier.
