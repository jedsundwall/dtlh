# Vermont Real Estate Cooperative (VREC)

**Where:** Burlington, VT (statewide membership)
**Founded:** Incorporated early 2020
**Legal form:** Vermont cooperative corporation (Title 11, Chapter 14)
**Why we care:** Recent, structurally analogous, and bylaws are public. VREC is a useful pressure-test for the cooperative path we ultimately decided not to take, and a good reference if our thinking ever shifts.

## Brief

VREC began organizing in 2019 and incorporated at the start of 2020. Founders include Matt Cropp of the Vermont Employee Ownership Center. The first property acquisition was in 2021 — a commercial building in Burlington tenanted by Good News Garage (a vehicle-donation nonprofit). Capital was raised after incorporation via $1,000 common shares sold only to Vermont residents under the state's intrastate exemption. By the time of first acquisition, VREC had ~40 members and ~$100K in equity, with a stated goal of up to $2M to acquire a 4–12 unit apartment building in Chittenden County.

Governance is one-member-one-vote regardless of share count, with a cap on shares per member. **Bylaws are intentionally public** — VREC says it will share them with anyone wanting to start a Vermont housing co-op (PDF posted on vrec.coop).

VREC has since coordinated with the Vermont Community Loan Fund to expand into supporting home ownership.

## What we're borrowing

- **Public bylaws as a starting template.** If our thinking ever shifts toward a cooperative structure, VREC's bylaws are the clean, recent, transparent starting point.
- **Single-state membership = intrastate exemption.** The simplicity of restricting membership to one state to avoid SEC registration is worth holding onto as a principle even within our nonprofit + LLC structure. Our small Class V (Voting) tranche is structured this way: WA residents only, small dollar amount, intrastate exemption.
- **One-member-one-vote regardless of share count.** A core cooperative principle that we are preserving in the Trust (members elect the board, each gets one vote) even though the LLC has economic shares with proportional rights.
- **Cap on shares per member.** Prevents concentration of control. Worth considering whether to apply something analogous on Class V interests in our LLC.

## What's different in our case

- **Vermont coop statute vs. Washington nonprofit + LLC.** VREC operates under Title 11, Chapter 14 of Vermont law, which has more developed cooperative-securities footing than Washington's parallel statutes. We're not a coop.
- **Scale and project type.** VREC's first deal was a small commercial building tenanted by a nonprofit. Their Chittenden County target was a 4–12 unit apartment building. We're contemplating a $5M+ acquisition leading to a $50M ground-up redevelopment.
- **Anchor structure.** VREC's $1,000 uniform-price member share doesn't accommodate accredited anchor investors writing $1M checks. Our Class A (Anchor) tranche addresses this gap.
